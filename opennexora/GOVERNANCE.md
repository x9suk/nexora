# OpenNexora Foundation Governance

This document describes the governance structure and processes of the OpenNexora Foundation.

## Governance Principles

The OpenNexora Foundation operates under the following principles:

1. **Openness**: All decisions are made openly with community input
2. **Transparency**: All processes and decisions are documented and accessible
3. **Meritocracy**: Contributions (code, documentation, community building) determine influence
4. **Consensus**: We strive for consensus-based decision making when possible
5. **Accountability**: All participants are responsible for upholding our values

## Board of Directors

The Board of Directors is responsible for:
- Setting the strategic direction of the Foundation
- Overseeing financial operations
- Ensuring legal and regulatory compliance
- Appointing Foundation officers
- Making decisions about Foundation membership

### Board Composition

- **Chairperson**: Elected by the Board
- **Executive Director**: Appointed by the Board
- **Technical Director**: Appointed by the Board
- **Community Director**: Elected by community members
- **Corporate Members**: Representatives from contributing companies

### Board Meetings

- The Board meets quarterly via video conference
- All meetings are open to Foundation members
- Minutes are published within 2 weeks of each meeting
- Decisions require a majority vote of the Board

## Technical Steering Committee (TSC)

The TSC is responsible for:
- Making technical decisions about Foundation projects
- Defining technical standards and best practices
- Coordinating cross-project initiatives
- Resolving technical disputes
- Managing project roadmaps

### TSC Composition

- **TSC Chair**: Elected by TSC members
- **Project Representatives**: One representative from each major project
- **Working Group Chairs**: Chairs of active Working Groups
- **Community Representatives**: Elected by community members

### TSC Meetings

- The TSC meets bi-weekly via video conference
- All meetings are open to the community
- Decisions are made by consensus when possible
- If consensus cannot be reached, decisions are made by majority vote
- Voting members include all TSC members

- Has a clear charter and scope
- Reports to the TSC
- Is led by a Chair and Co-Chair
- Has open membership

### Creating a New Working Group

To create a new Working Group:
1. Submit a proposal to the TSC
2. The proposal must include:
   - Charter and scope
   - Initial membership
   - Proposed timeline and milestones
   - Resource requirements
3. The TSC reviews and votes on the proposal
4. If approved, the Working Group is formed and announced

## Decision Making Process

### Consensus Building

We strive for consensus in all decisions. The process:
1. **Proposal**: Any community member can propose a change
2. **Discussion**: Open discussion period (minimum 2 weeks)
3. **Revision**: Proposal is revised based on feedback
4. **Consensus**: If no objections remain, consensus is achieved
5. **Implementation**: Change is implemented

### Voting

When consensus cannot be reached:
1. **Motion**: A formal motion is made
2. **Discussion**: Final discussion period (1 week)
3. **Vote**: Voting period (1 week)
4. **Result**: Majority vote determines outcome

### Quorum

- Board meetings: 50% of Board members
- TSC meetings: 60% of TSC members
- Working Group meetings: 50% of Working Group members

## Conflict Resolution

Conflicts are resolved through the following process:
1. **Direct Communication**: Parties attempt to resolve directly
2. **Working Group Mediation**: Working Group Chair mediates
3. **TSC Mediation**: TSC mediates if unresolved
4. **Board Decision**: Board makes final decision if needed

## Changes to Governance

Changes to this governance document require:
1. A proposal submitted to the TSC
2. A 30-day comment period
3. Approval by 75% of the Board of Directors
4. A 14-day final review period before taking effect

## Amendments

This document may be amended by the Board of Directors with approval of 75% of Board members. All amendments are announced to the community with at least 30 days notice.
